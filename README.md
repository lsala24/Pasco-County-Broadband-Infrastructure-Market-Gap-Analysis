## Project Overview
This project provides a strategic spatial audit of broadband accessibility and infrastructure gaps within Pasco County, Florida. By intersecting demographic data with existing service footprints, the analysis identifies underserved "digital deserts" and high-priority zones for future infrastructure investment and commercial expansion.

## Technical Objectives
* **Service Gap Analysis:** Mapping existing broadband coverage to identify census blocks with limited or no access to high-speed fiber or cable.
* **Demographic Correlation:** Overlaying socioeconomic indicators (income, education, and age) with service data to identify communities at risk of digital exclusion.
* **Market Opportunity Mapping:** Pinpointing high-density residential and commercial areas that are currently underserved, providing a roadmap for utility expansion.

## Key Skills Demonstrated
* **Network & Infrastructure Mapping:** Analyzing linear and nodal utility assets within a regional spatial framework.
* **Socioeconomic Data Synthesis:** Utilizing American Community Survey (ACS) and FCC Form 477 data to drive infrastructure decision-making.
* **Strategic Storytelling:** Using ArcGIS StoryMaps to communicate complex technical gaps to stakeholders, policy makers, and investors.

## Data Sources
* **Federal Communications Commission (FCC):** Broadband deployment data and service provider footprints.
* **U.S. Census Bureau:** Demographic and socioeconomic attribute data.
* **Pasco County GIS:** Municipal boundaries and existing utility corridor data.
